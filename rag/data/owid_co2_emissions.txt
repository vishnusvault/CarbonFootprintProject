INDIA-SPECIFIC BREAKDOWN (2022)

India's total CO2 emissions: approximately 2.8 billion tonnes CO2
- Electricity & heat: 49% of total
- Industry: 23%
- Transport: 13%
- Buildings: 8%
- Other: 7%

India's emissions have grown by approximately 6% per year over the past decade, driven by rapid economic development. However, per-capita emissions remain among the lowest of major economies.

Daily CO2 equivalent:
- India average: 5.2 kg CO2e/day (1.9 tCO2e/year ÷ 365)
- Global average: 12.9 kg CO2e/day (4.7 tCO2e/year ÷ 365)
- USA average: 40.8 kg CO2e/day

LIFESTYLE AND CONSUMPTION EMISSIONS

Research by Ivanova et al. (2020) "Quantifying the potential for climate change mitigation of consumption options" in Environmental Research Letters quantified the CO2-saving potential of individual lifestyle changes:

High impact actions (>1 tCO2e/year saved):
- Having one fewer child: 58.6 tCO2e/year (aggregate, not personal lifestyle)
- Living car-free: 2.4 tCO2e/year saved
- Avoiding one long-haul flight per year: 1.5–2.0 tCO2e/year
- Shifting to a plant-based diet: 0.5–1.5 tCO2e/year

Medium impact (0.1–1 tCO2e/year saved):
- Buying green electricity: 1.47 tCO2e/year (where coal grid)
- Switching to an electric car: 1.0–1.5 tCO2e/year (India)
- Reducing home heating: 0.3 tCO2e/year
- Buying energy efficient appliances: 0.3 tCO2e/year

Lower impact (>0.1 tCO2e/year):
- Reducing food waste by 50%: 0.08 tCO2e/year
- Recycling properly: 0.06 tCO2e/year
- Line-drying clothes: 0.05 tCO2e/year

HISTORICAL TRENDS

Global CO2 emissions from fossil fuels have increased from approximately 6 GtCO2 in 1950 to 37 GtCO2 in 2023. The 2020 COVID-19 pandemic caused a 5.4% decline, the largest annual drop ever recorded. Emissions rebounded strongly in 2021 and 2022.

The top 5 emitters account for approximately 62% of global CO2:
1. China: 32%
2. United States: 13%
3. India: 7%
4. Russia: 5%
5. Japan: 3%

CARBON EQUIVALENTS (Useful Metaphors)

These equivalents help make abstract CO2 numbers tangible:
- 1 kg CO2e = driving approximately 5.5 km in a petrol car
- 1 kg CO2e = burning approximately 0.4 litres of petrol
- 1 kg CO2e = a tree absorbing CO2 for approximately 2 weeks
- 100 kg CO2e = the carbon sequestered by about 1.4 trees in a year
- 1 tCO2e = driving approximately 5,500 km in a petrol car

Tree sequestration rates:
- A young tree sequesters approximately 6 kg CO2/year
- A mature tree sequesters approximately 22 kg CO2/year
- A hectare of forest sequesters approximately 5,500 kg CO2/year

Flight equivalents (one-way, economy):
- Mumbai → Delhi (1,150 km): 293 kg CO2e → equivalent to driving 1,628 km
- Chennai → Mumbai (1,328 km): 338 kg CO2e → equivalent to 3.4 weeks of average Indian per-capita emissions
- Delhi → London (6,700 km): 1,307 kg CO2e → equivalent to 251 days of average Indian per-capita emissions
