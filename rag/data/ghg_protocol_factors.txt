Source: World Resources Institute / World Business Council for Sustainable Development

OVERVIEW OF GHG PROTOCOL EMISSION FACTORS

The GHG Protocol provides the world's most widely used greenhouse gas accounting standards. The emission factors below are drawn from the Cross-Sector Tools and are consistent with IPCC AR6 Global Warming Potential values (GWP100).

All values are in kgCO2e per unit (including CO2, CH4, and N2O converted to CO2 equivalent using GWP100).

TRANSPORT EMISSION FACTORS

Road Transport:
- Petrol/gasoline passenger car (average): 0.180 kgCO2e/km (lifecycle including fuel production)
- Diesel passenger car (average): 0.171 kgCO2e/km
- Electric vehicle (India grid 0.82 kgCO2/kWh, typical EV 0.065 kWh/km): 0.053 kgCO2e/km
- Diesel urban bus (per passenger): 0.089 kgCO2e/km
- Electric metro rail (India grid): 0.041 kgCO2e/passenger-km
- Diesel intercity train (India): 0.041 kgCO2e/passenger-km

Aviation:
- Short-haul flights (<3700 km, economy class): 0.255 kgCO2e/passenger-km
  (includes Radiative Forcing Index multiplier of 1.9 for non-CO2 climate effects at altitude)
- Long-haul flights (>3700 km, economy class): 0.195 kgCO2e/passenger-km
  (RFI multiplier applied; long-haul more fuel-efficient per km than short-haul)
- Business class multiplier: approximately 2.9× economy class (seat width, cargo allocation)
- First class multiplier: approximately 4× economy class

Key aviation routes and their CO2e emissions (economy, one-way):
- Chennai to Mumbai (1,328 km, short-haul): 338.6 kg CO2e
- Delhi to Bangalore (1,730 km, short-haul): 441.2 kg CO2e
- Mumbai to London (7,192 km, long-haul): 1,402.4 kg CO2e

ENERGY EMISSION FACTORS

Electricity grid factors (kgCO2e/kWh):
- India national average (CEA 2024): 0.820
- United States national average (EPA eGRID 2022): 0.386
- European Union average (IEA 2023): 0.233
- United Kingdom (BEIS 2023): 0.233
- China (IEA 2023): 0.560
- Global average: 0.436

Stationary combustion:
- LPG (liquefied petroleum gas): 1.560 kgCO2e/litre (including CH4 and N2O)
- Diesel fuel: 2.68 kgCO2e/litre
- Petrol/gasoline: 2.31 kgCO2e/litre
- Natural gas: 0.202 kgCO2e/kWh (or 2.03 kgCO2e/m³)
- Diesel generator (average load): 2.60 kgCO2e/hour

FOOD AND LAND USE

Per meal lifecycle emissions (including land use change, farming, processing, transport):
- Beef-heavy meal: 6.0 kgCO2e per meal (range 4–10 depending on production system)
- Mixed omnivore meal: 3.5 kgCO2e per meal
- Vegetarian meal: 2.0 kgCO2e per meal
- Vegan meal: 1.5 kgCO2e per meal

Source for food factors: Poore & Nemecek (2018), "Reducing food's environmental impacts through producers and consumers," Science, 360(6392).

Key food comparisons:
- 1 kg beef: 60 kgCO2e | 1 kg chicken: 6.9 kgCO2e | 1 kg tofu: 3.0 kgCO2e | 1 kg lentils: 0.9 kgCO2e
- Swapping from beef to chicken for one meal per week saves approximately 156 kgCO2e per year
- Going vegetarian saves approximately 0.5 tCO2e per year vs. average omnivore diet

CONSUMER GOODS

Electronics (lifecycle, including manufacturing, use, end-of-life):
- Smartphone: 70 kgCO2e (manufacturing dominates — ~80% of lifetime impact)
- Laptop: 300–400 kgCO2e
- Television (55"): 400–600 kgCO2e
- Small electronics (earphones, tablet): 30 kgCO2e (average)
- Large electronics (TV, washing machine): 200 kgCO2e (average)

Clothing:
- Average clothing item (cotton t-shirt to synthetic jacket): 10 kgCO2e per item
- A pair of jeans: 33 kgCO2e (including cotton production, dyeing, transport)
- Buying second-hand clothing reduces emissions by ~65–70%

CARBON BUDGET CONTEXT

The remaining 1.5°C carbon budget (as of 2020) is approximately 400–500 GtCO2. At current global emission rates of ~37 GtCO2/year, this budget will be exhausted in approximately 11–14 years without major reductions.

India's fair share of the remaining budget (population-based) is approximately 50–80 GtCO2, requiring per-capita emissions to stay below approximately 3.5 tCO2e/year through 2050.
